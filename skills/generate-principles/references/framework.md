# Principles Framework

Use this reference when the agent needs the reasoning model behind `generate-principles`.

## Research Basis

Ray Dalio's framework treats principles as reusable decision rules built from reality, mistakes, reflection, and root-cause analysis. The useful loop is:

1. Notice reality, pain, mistake, conflict, or repeated behavior.
2. Reflect until the root cause is concrete.
3. Convert the lesson into a decision rule.
4. Reuse the rule in similar future situations.
5. Update or retire the rule when evidence contradicts it.

Atomic Habits turns the rule into a system. James Clear's model emphasizes identity-based habits, the habit loop, environment design, and the Four Laws of Behavior Change: make it obvious, attractive, easy, and satisfying.

## Synthesis For This Skill

A principle without a habit system is often just a thought. A habit without a principle can become mechanical and disconnected from identity.

This skill combines both:

- Dalio layer: What did reality teach Rami?
- Principle layer: What reusable decision rule follows from that?
- Atomic Habits layer: What identity, cue, response, reward, and environment design will make the principle livable?

## Strong Principle Test

A principle is strong when it passes these checks:

- It is based on something that happened or repeatedly appears in the notes.
- It says what to do when a specific situation appears.
- It explains why the behavior matters.
- It names the failure mode it prevents.
- It can be practiced through a small behavior.
- It has a review cadence so it can improve.

If any check fails, score it lower and keep it as a candidate.
